<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
Blackwater and Erik Prince the merchants of death
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5613843506316239829' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="float: left; margin-right: 1em; text-align: left;"><tbody>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ACHOntAD810/UsR4dh9NfDI/AAAAAAAAIxA/0PLUhxx9gtY/s1600/fallujah-birth-defect1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><img border="0" height="240" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ACHOntAD810/UsR4dh9NfDI/AAAAAAAAIxA/0PLUhxx9gtY/s320/fallujah-birth-defect1.jpg" width="320" /></a></td></tr>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">Fallujah baby, a gift from the US taxpayer. Is that what we wanted?</td></tr>
</tbody></table>
<div class="MsoNormal">
left: deformed Fallujah child from US chemical weapon use.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
by Richard Mellor

Afscme Local 444, retired<br />
<br />
After serving 12 years, the US government just released
three Chinese Muslims, the final&nbsp; batch of more than two-dozen that were held at Guantanamo Bay, the US
concentration camp in occupied Cuba. The Muslims, ethnic Uighurs were never
charged with anything and cleared for release by a judge in 2008.*</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
We don&#8217;t have to dig too deeply to get a sense of why young
Muslims Arabs or other victims of US government aggression are drawn to <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">&#8220;terrorist&#8221;</i> groups or are engaged in
attacks on US personnel or property in one form or another. Resistance to
foreign domination takes many forms.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
We could start with one of the world&#8217;s most renowned
terrorists and mass murderers, Erik Prince. Prince is the founder and CEO of
the mercenary outfit we once knew as Blackwater USA.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Like Osama bin Laden, Erik Prince is a religious man, a good
Catholic (might excommunication be in order Francis?).<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>He was born with a silver spoon in his mouth
wanting for nothing as his father made billions in the auto parts business, a
lucrative venture in Michigan. But such mundane manufacture was not for Erik;
he needed action.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>At college he was a volunteer
fireman and a rescue diver, he could have taken another direction and put his
skills to work in some form of social service, but sons of billionaires don&#8217;t
do that.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
After his parents died, Erik left the service, sold the
family business, and ventured out on his own, with a nice gift of cash he never
earned of course.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>He started with a
private military training center in rural North Carolina that was to become
Blackwater USA which was incorporated in 2002.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span><a href="http://www.businessweek.com/articles/2013-11-21/what-blackwater-founder-erik-prince-learned-about-the-business-of-war">Business
Week points out</a> that Blackwater went from training soldiers (and law
enforcement) to finding them work.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>War
is good for the mercenary business and Prince prospered, providing security for
US convoys, personnel and politicians and doing covert work for the CIA,
perhaps the world&#8217;s most efficient terrorist organization. It was the events of
911 that really helped as the CIA approached Prince about supplying mercenaries
for the Afghanistan war.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
This led to more contracts and Blackwater became the main
supplier of security to many government departments and agencies as well as
individuals.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Two of Blackwater&#8217;s
contractors were assigned to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Bremer">Paul
Bremer</a> the Cromwell of Iraq who ran the country from May 2004 through June
2005.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Business Week points out that one
of these mercenaries was a guy named Frank Gallagher who was a former security
head for another notorious mass murderer, Henry Kissinger.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Things got even better for Blackwater:<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span><i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">&#8220;When
the Pentagon decided to contract out the whole detail in August 2003, he became
its leader. Seven days later, Blackwater had found 34 more guys&#8212;former Army
Rangers and special forces, Force Recon marines, SEALs, and SWAT team
officers&#8212;promised them $600 a day, and flown them into Baghdad.&#8221;</i>
(BusinessWeek)</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Blackwater eventually won the contract to provide security
for all US government&#8217;s personnel in Iraq.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>In all Prince and Blackwater received close to $1 billion in government or more accurately, US taxpayer money.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>The reader should
think about this for a minute.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Prince is
not only a conservative Catholic, he was an passionate believer <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">&#8220;&#8230;in the dynamism of the private sector.&#8221;</i>
Says BW, and that, <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">&#8220;&#8230;some of the world&#8217;s
most frustrating problems&#8212;piracy, warlords, genocide&#8212;could be solved by small
groups of highly trained men with guns.&#8221;<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span></i>Funny how Libertarians (right wing Republicans in reality) have no
problem getting rich off taxpayer subsidies as they champion the private sector
over the public. Ross Perot made his money the same way through government
(Medicare) contracts.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Yet they all want
to eliminate social services, public sector jobs and our pensions.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Blackwater we know got in to some serious trouble in
Iraq.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>There was the whole Fallujah
affair. Four mercenaries were killed by Iraqi&#8217;s and their bodies mutilated.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>For this, the US taxpayer came to the rescue
again in the form of the imbecile George W Bush who sent in the Marines, twice.
The destruction and chemical warfare the US government inflicted on the people
of Fallujah will never be forgotten.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>This city will forever remain a heroic shrine to the resistance of the
Iraqi people to an invasion by the most powerful military ever constructed. A
Google search will give the reader a glimpse in to the horrific results of the
US war on this city, retaliation for resistance (see links at the end of this
commentary).<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>The children are still
being born deformed as are the children of Vietnam from previous US chemical warfare. Most Americans put these issues out of our minds, but the
Iraqi, Arab and Muslim people cannot. I recall here my mother telling me that
with the bombings in London in WW2 if an ejected German pilot were to have
landed in her neighborhood he would have been torn to pieces.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>That&#8217;s what the Iraqi&#8217;s did to the Blackwater
mercenaries; a rare chance at payback but they suffered for it thanks to Bush
and co. War is not nice</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Blackwater we know has changed its name but the guilty still
walk free. The families of the slain mercenaries of Fallujah sued the company
and won some settlement.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>The murder of
innocent Iraqi&#8217;s in Nisour Square in 2007 and the killing of a bodyguard for
the Iraqi president in 2006 by a drunken Blackwater mercenary, led to more
problems although he was never charged with any crime. I wonder if that angers
Iraqi&#8217;s to the point that they might harm innocent Americans.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Perhaps it does.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
As BusinessWeek points out, Blackwater has faced numerous
lawsuits from many US government departments and groups as well as the
relatives of victims murdered in Nisour Square. There are misplaced weapons and
missing funds although a jury cleared executives of any wrongdoings this year. </div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Prince is still free and involved in other ventures and linked
to the <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;"><span style="font-family: Times;">&#8220;Puntland
Maritime Police Force, a paramilitary force trained by South African advisers
to fight piracy.&#8221;</span></i><span style="font-family: Times;"> Says BW.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;&nbsp; </span>He lives in Abu Dhabi, a slave state in the
Middle East that imports all its labor from poor countries.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Prince has been involved in security
operations to protect the thugs that rule these countries but according to
reports is backed by the US government in that venture.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>The maintenance of strong dictators that
support US foreign policy is crucial.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>He
can also earn some extra cash from his ownership of the name <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">&#8220;Blackwater&#8221; </i>through the sale of
Blackwater knives, pellet guns and even a <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">&#8220;first-person
shooter video game&#8221;. </i>He is a man of conscience though, the game is rigged
so the shooter, that nice young 12 year old next door, can&#8217;t shoot
civilians.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Unfortunately, in many of the
regions where the US government treads, there is no state militia or military,
all the freedom fighters and those that resist occupation are civilians. Tanks,
planes, boats and uniforms are expensive.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>He also has an estate in Virginia. </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Times;">As Business Week puts it, <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">&#8220;These days, Prince&#8217;s day job is running the
Frontier Resource Group, a fund that invests in natural resources in Africa.&#8221;</i></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Times;">Heaven help them.</span></div>
