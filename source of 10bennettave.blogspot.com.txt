<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, January 3, 2014</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://theskateboardmag.com/content/blogs/bart/wallofdeath.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='2276638054486960095' itemprop='blogId'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://10bennettave.blogspot.com/2014/01/hippies-vs-punks-richard-linda.html'>Hippies vs. Punks: Richard & Linda Thompson's "Light(s)" Albums, Pt. 3, Shoot Out the Lights</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7707802814032065061' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/1G9Xdp6oY_I/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/1G9Xdp6oY_I&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/1G9Xdp6oY_I&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
There is one run I have been doing for over 15 years now. I call it the "Big Man's Run" because it involves running for over an hour. When I was in my thirties I could complete it in one hour. Now, it takes me an hour and a quarter. The Big Man's Run (BMR) is a little under eight miles and takes me north out of my Capitol Hill neighborhood over the University Bridge and then onto the <a href="http://www.seattle.gov/parks/burkegilman/bgtrail.htm">Burke-Gilman Trail</a>&nbsp;until I get to Fremont; at which point I exit the trail, cross the Fremont Bridge and then head east down the hill to hook up with Westlake Avenue, more exactly, the miles of parking lot that rim Lake Union. I head south until I get to the South Lake Union Cascade neighborhood, which used to be a funky, bohemian low-rent district but is now dominated by new concrete architecture that anchors the Amazon commercial empire. Then I head east climbing hills and a freeway overpass until I am back in my Capitol Hill neighborhood.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://theskateboardmag.com/content/blogs/bart/wallofdeath.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="287" src="http://theskateboardmag.com/content/blogs/bart/wallofdeath.jpg" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
On the BMR, right when I cross the University Bridge and am connecting with the Burke-Gilman Trail, there is a <a href="http://www.seattleweekly.com/2008-09-17/calendar/the-wall-of-death/">sculpture</a> that incorporates the words "The Wall of Death." It is located, incongruously, beneath the north end of University Bridge where the Burke-Gilman Trail passes underneath. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wall_of_death_(carnival_sideshow)">The Wall of Death</a> was a once-popular carnival sideshow where stunt-riding motorcyclists ride around a circular wall climbing higher and higher as their centripetal force grows. (A variation of The Wall of Death <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/video/movies/100000002133035/inside-the-place-beyond-the-pines.html">opens</a> the 2013 Derek Cianfrance film <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Place_Beyond_the_Pines"><i>The Place Beyond the Pines</i></a>.) The Wall of Death sculpture beneath the University Bridge might be so named because it is a popular spot for skateboarders attracted by the forty-five-degree angle concrete walls.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.fecalface.com/blogs/trippe/9-18-06/DSC_8329.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="265" src="http://www.fecalface.com/blogs/trippe/9-18-06/DSC_8329.jpg" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Whenever I run past the sculpture, which, as I said, I've been doing regularly for a long time, I can't help but think of the final track on&nbsp;<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_and_Linda_Thompson">Richard</a>&nbsp;&amp;&nbsp;<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linda_Thompson_(singer)">Linda Thompson</a>'s&nbsp;break-up album, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shoot_Out_the_Lights"><i>Shoot Out the Lights</i></a> (1982).<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/YH_8VINpfKQ/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/YH_8VINpfKQ&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/YH_8VINpfKQ&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
<i>Shoot Out the Lights</i> is widely acknowledged as Richard &amp; Linda Thompson's masterpiece. For instance, it comes in at number <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/500-greatest-albums-of-all-time-20120531/richard-and-linda-thompson-shoot-out-the-lights-20120524">332</a> on <i>Rolling Stone</i>'s <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/500-greatest-albums-of-all-time-20120531">500 Greatest Albums of All Time</a>, significantly outpolling <i><a href="http://10bennettave.blogspot.com/2013/12/hippies-vs-punks-richard-linda.html">I Want to See the Bright Lights Tonight</a></i> (1974) which comes in at 471.<br />
<br />
I prefer <i>Bright Lights</i> and <i><a href="http://10bennettave.blogspot.com/2013/12/hippies-vs-punks-richard-linda_27.html">First Light</a></i> (1978) of the three great "Light(s)" albums of Richard &amp; Linda Thompson. But thanks to the success of <i>Shoot Out the Lights</i> -- it was a critics choice album back in 1982 boosted by Robert Christgau's '<b>A</b>' grade in <i>The Village Voice</i> -- it established Richard Thompson on a long, productive solo career that is still going strong, and it sparked a much-deserved reappraisal of the couple's previous recordings.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/lX9iavUiLY4/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/lX9iavUiLY4&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/lX9iavUiLY4&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
Recorded quickly in the summer of 1981 while Linda was pregnant, <i>Shoot Out the Lights</i> was produced by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Boyd">Joe Boyd</a>&nbsp;(the guy responsible for discovering and recording <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fairport_Convention">Fairport Convention</a> back in the 1960s) for his small <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hannibal_Records">Hannibal Records</a> label. Richard &amp; Linda Thompson had been dropped by Chrysalis because of the poor sales of <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunnyvista">Sunnyvista</a></i> (1979). By the time the album is released in March 1982, the Thompson's marriage had collapsed.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/E5zKKNbdcJM/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/E5zKKNbdcJM&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/E5zKKNbdcJM&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
An earlier version of the album exists in bootleg form. Recorded by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gerry_Rafferty">Gerry Rafferty</a>&nbsp;in September and October of 1980 and unsuccessfully shopped to record labels, it is a slicker studio album; some of its tracks can be found online under the heading "Rafferty's Folly," like this version of "Don't Renege on Our Love":<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/ZbdalXbEYeo/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/ZbdalXbEYeo&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/ZbdalXbEYeo&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
For me, my memories of <i>Shoot Out the Lights</i> always begin with the first side of the album, particularly the first three cuts: "Don't Renege on Our Love," "Walking on a Wire," and "Man in Need." These songs are a point-counterpoint of an aggrieved wife and husband. I would listen to this album before heading off to work a second-shift job on the 99th floor of the World Trade Center. My own marriage was collapsing that sunny spring. I believe, looking back now, that <i>Shoot Out the Lights</i> provided excellent counsel:<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/7o3h7eyVRp8/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/7o3h7eyVRp8&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/7o3h7eyVRp8&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/LxmGtWhFcH0/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/LxmGtWhFcH0&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/LxmGtWhFcH0&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>

<tr><td bgcolor="#ffffff" colspan="2" height="5"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">In both Saturday games the home team is favored by two-and-a-half points.
Kansas City travels to Indianapolis for the first game. And you would
think this would be a no-brainer since the Colts dominated the Chiefs at
Arrowhead a couple weeks back. But for some reason <b style="background-color: transparent;">I'm going with Kansas
City</b>. I think the Chiefs reverse their second-half-of-the-season slide and
find some focus and mental toughness. I think speedsters <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamaal_Charles" style="background-color: transparent;">Jamaal Charles</a>
and <a href="http://www.dextermccluster.com/" style="background-color: transparent;">Dexter McCluster</a> find sure footing on Lucas Oil Stadium turf, and
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brandon_Flowers_(American_football)" style="background-color: transparent;">Brandon Flowers</a> contains <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T._Y._Hilton" style="background-color: transparent;">T.Y. Hilton</a>. So long as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alex_Smith" style="background-color: transparent;">Alex Smith</a> doesn't throw
a bunch of interceptions Kansas City rolls on.
</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">The Saturday evening game should be a humdinger. The Saints are not known
for their prowess on the road. But you know <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drew_Brees" style="background-color: transparent;">Drew Brees</a> is going to pile up
the yards against the Eagles defense if last week Dallas backup <a href="http://www.dallascowboys.com/team/roster/Kyle-Orton/f708dee3-8a22-452e-8bad-53321362b9ca" style="background-color: transparent;">Kyle Orton</a>
was able to throw for over 350 yards and two TDs. I don't see Rob Ryan's defense being able to contain <a href="http://www.leseanmccoy.com/" style="background-color: transparent;">LeSean McCoy</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Foles" style="background-color: transparent;">Nick Foles</a>. <b style="background-color: transparent;">Philadelphia should win in a shoot-out</b>.
</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">The early game Sunday has San Diego visiting Cincinnati. The Chargers have
proven they can beat anybody this year by knocking off both Indianapolis
and Denver. Couple this with the fact that <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marvin_Lewis" style="background-color: transparent;">Marvin Lewis</a>' Bengals are 0-4
in the playoffs and you can imagine a situation where the seven-point
underdog Chargers pull off an upset. But I don't see it happening. What's
different about this Bengals team is the depth on both offense and
defense. <b style="background-color: transparent;">Take Cincinnati</b>.
</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">The premiere match-up of the wild card round is the Sunday afternoon game
in Green Bay. The Packers can knock off the 49ers. Aaron Rodgers and
Randall Cobb are back in the lineup, and Eddie Lacy and John Starks are a
potent rushing combination. The weather is supposed to be freezing. It's
hard to go against San Francisco. The 49ers have been on a roll lately;
along with Denver, they've been playing the best football in the league. But <b style="background-color: transparent;">I like Green Bay in this one</b>. The Packers have a habit of
underperforming when they are favorites going into the playoffs but
surpassing expectations when they are underdogs.</span></td></tr>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://10bennettave.blogspot.com/2014/01/geneva-ii.html'>Geneva II</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1586682459804702481' itemprop='description articleBody'>
The Geneva II peace talks on Syria begin in three weeks and a lot is in play in the Middle East.&nbsp;Majid bin Muhammad al-Majid, head of the Al Qaeda-affiliated Abdullah Azzam Brigades and a Saudi, has been taken into custody. The Adbullah Azzam Brigades had claimed responsibility for the November suicide bombing of the Iranian Embassy in Beirut, and they were suspected of firing a missile into northern Israel on Sunday. Anne Barnard has the story, "<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/01/02/world/middleeast/lebanon.html?ref=world&amp;_r=0">Lebanon Arrests Leader of Qaeda-Linked Group, Reports Say</a>":<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
An Iranian national security official, Alaeddin Boroujerdi, on Wednesday praised the Lebanese security forces for apprehending Mr. Majid, and blamed him for the embassy bombing. He also urged the Lebanese government to consider the fact that &#8220;the main element in the operation is of Saudi nationality,&#8221; Iran&#8217;s semiofficial Fars News Agency reported.</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
***&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
The Iranian official, Mr. Boroujerdi, said that Lebanese security forces had arrested two people, including someone involved in &#8220;the assassination of the Hezbollah leader in Lebanon,&#8221; an apparent reference to the shooting death in December of <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/05/world/middleeast/hezbollah-leader-is-assassinated-in-beirut.html">Hassane Laqees</a>, a senior Hezbollah militant. It was unclear whether he was blaming the Abdullah Azzam Brigades or Saudi Arabia for killing Mr. Laqees. Citing the killers&#8217; professionalism, Hezbollah had blamed the hit on Israel rather than Sunni jihadist groups.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Mr. Majid&#8217;s detention was potentially sensitive in a divided Lebanon, especially under a caretaker government that has been in place for months because of political stalemate. The army has tried to maintain its reputation as the only largely neutral security agency, even as it remains too weak to challenge Hezbollah&#8217;s independent militia, and Lebanese Sunnis increasingly see it as leaning toward the Shiite party.</blockquote>
The Iranians are still barred from attending Geneva II, ostensibly because they won't sign off on Geneva I and the concept of a Baathist-opposition transitional government with full executive powers, even though it is broadly accepted that no deal is possible without Iranian participation. Aron Lund breaks it down in "<a href="http://carnegieendowment.org/syriaincrisis/?fa=54046">Can Iran Go to Geneva II Without Endorsing Geneva I?</a>" on his <a href="http://carnegieendowment.org/syriaincrisis/?fa=experts&amp;id=913">Syria in Crisis</a> blog. Lund seems to think that endorsing Geneva I is meaningless and that Iran could do so at anytime without compromising its bargaining position. He seems to think that Iran is holding out for further concessions by the United States.<br />
<br />
One thing that jumped out at me from Lund's piece is the large number of nations attending Geneva II:<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Apart from Syria&#8217;s government and opposition, the <a href="http://www.un.org/sg/offthecuff/index.asp?nid=3212">Geneva II guest list</a> includes representatives of the United Nations, the Arab League, the European Union, the Organization of Islamic Cooperation, the United States, Russia, France, the United Kingdom, China, Algeria, Brazil, Canada, Denmark, Egypt, Germany, India, Indonesia, Iraq, Italy, Japan, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Morocco, Norway, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Turkey, and the United Arab Emirates.</blockquote>
The more countries attending, the more likely it is the event will be nothing but ceremonial, a performance for the cameras. This does not preclude that possibility that a substantive agreement can be reached at a meeting that is not part of the formally recognized mega-gathering and then brought to the larger group for a sign-off. It just seems like a long shot to me.

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://10bennettave.blogspot.com/2014/01/the-colt-45-chronicle-50.html'>The Colt 45 Chronicle #50</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6361475942908855119' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="tr_bq">
My father-in-law, to whom this letter is addressed, and I never had a particularly warm relationship. My wife adored him. He was a Hippie, sort of a Marlboro Man version -- Western, laconic; a painter, who taught art &nbsp;in Southern Oregon first at the college and then later at the community college. He was basically a good guy, just shy and emotionally walled off. Selfish.</div>
<br />
The letter below relates, in the first paragraph, an afternoon spent looking for work when I arrived in New York City and my thoughts, strange though they were to relate to one's father-in-law, on the bankruptcy of romantic love.<br />
<br />
The second paragraph is a romantic remembrance of the apartment -- referred to here by its street address, 2210 -- I shared with my wife in Berkeley.<br />
<blockquote>
<span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">Autumn 1988</span></blockquote>
<blockquote>
<span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">Pete,</span></blockquote>
<blockquote>
<span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">Bought myself a monkey suit the other day, and wore it today, while I did a little slick-hair-&amp;-dandruff jig through Wall Street and then Madison Avenue. Bought myself a beer for lunch at a nearby Irish pub while I was waiting for the next installment on my road to success. Budweiser -- not Harp or Bass -- was my choice; got out my book, a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Wycherly">Wycherley</a> play freshly purchased from a rug on the sidewalk (where everything is sold), tossed my head up at the TV and caught a few snippets of American Legion Baseball (ESPN); some lanky teen was lobbing a breaking ball over the plate, and getting banged for doing it. I dropped down&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">for a line or two more of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Country_Wife">THE COUNTRY WIFE.</a> When, bam! -- It hit me, something very exact: "No," the character Harcourt says, "mistresses are like books. If you pore upon them too much, they doze you and make you unfit for company; but if used discreetly, you are fitter for conversation by 'em." Now, either&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">this is saying that monogamy breeds misogyny, or, the simple one, "Too much of a good thing . . . therefore, moderation." Either way, it affected me, affected me in that if this is truly the case, which I knew exactly at that point it was, Oh, boy, how shitty and really less than ideal romantic, hyper-sexual "perfect relationship" love is. So I thought about this, for the span of a beer mug, basking in the simultaneity of Harcourt's statement and Legionnaire baseball, and then I pulled Ashley's watch out of my pocket and glanced at the&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">time. It was time to go. I entertained the idea of staying where I was and not moving an inch except for motioning to the barkeep to graduate me to a pint of Bass, but I was in the monkey suit, and my conscience bit me. I slid off the stool with my tail between my legs and scampered off to interview letter 'X.'</span></blockquote>
<blockquote>
<span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">The one thing about today which I can't turn my back on, monkey suit, noose and all, is that it wasn't Berkeley. No more waxing, ruminating and peregrinating in anticipation of the parting of clouds and sounding of trumpets as the masses spill into the river Jordan, pounding on my apartment door (#11). Too many fantasies and too much life's blood gets lapped up by your own four walls, which doesn't mean that you can't live a "good" life, just not a very satisfying one. (So many fucking dreams don't square up outside of home; that's why we have so many self-mutilators: beer, whiskey, coyote's breath, speed, criminals.) And isn't it odd that out of this Icarian potpourri some get tagged artists, writers, prophets? -- World historic individuals, as Hegel would say.) But Pete, the real reason for this letter is to thank you, for helping us shut down old 2210 in proper fashion. We had a lot good evenings with beer, and a lot of good mornings with coffee. And&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">out of everybody who was in and out of that place, friends and family, I always felt that you came the closest to feeling the appreciation for it that I felt for it. Those last nights with you and Jesse (and maybe this is too backward looking and romantic of me) were the absolute apotheosis of what 2210 always was. And I'm talking here of the yellow light and Navajo walls -- and your paintings -- and the books, and beer in the fridge, and some music playing, and an important conversation on somebody's lips. But for all this, it was time to call it quits.</span></blockquote>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://10bennettave.blogspot.com/2013/12/two-perfect-songs-for-new-years-eve.html'>Two Perfect Songs for New Year's Eve</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1000870533606818973' itemprop='description articleBody'>
The ride in on the bus this morning of New Year's Eve I was treated to "Head-And-Feet-Only-Man" which shuffled on my iPod.&nbsp;"Head-And-Feet-Only-Man" is off <a href="http://www.hootpage.com/">Mike Watt</a>'s 2011 album <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyphenated-man"><i>Hyphenated-Man</i></a>, an homage to the paintings of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hieronymous_Bosch">Hieronymus Bosch</a>.<br />
<div>
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/NaVbDkmA7j0/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/NaVbDkmA7j0&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="http://youtube.googleapis.com/v/NaVbDkmA7j0&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
"Head-And-Feet-Only-Man" is the perfect way to begin one's workday, any workday but particularly the last one of the year. "What a fucked up thing it is!" I don't think <i>Hyphenated-Man</i> has received the attention it deserves. It is a true Punk record.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Then after a suitably shitty day, a day where low spirits began to compress into anger, which gradually dissipated on the train ride home, another selection off <i>Hyphenated-Man</i> shuffled on my iPod as I strode up the escalator out of the station on my way into the night air, "Man-Shitting-Man":</div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://10bennettave.blogspot.com/2013/12/house-of-saud-destabilizes-lebanon.html'>House of Saud Destabilizes Lebanon</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8450286156314553614' itemprop='description articleBody'>
While taking in the final Sunday of regular season NFL action I finally got around to reading Dahr Jamail's devastating summary <a href="http://www.tomdispatch.com/blog/175785/tomgram%3A_dahr_jamail%2C_the_climate_change_scorecard/">piece</a> on the current predictions of some climate scientists who see a massive dying coming our way in the not-to-distant future because of huge releases of trapped methane and carbon as the Arctic permafrost thaws.<br />
<br />
The dystopia presented by Jamail is a world where the places food is now grown will no longer be able to support crops. Most people will die and those remaining will migrate north and south to the Arctic and Antarctica. It is a dire forecast but one that is grounded in science. The only way to avoid further warming is to have a reduction in growth, something antithetical to the global capitalist order. So get ready for dystopia.<br />
<br />
What is being peddled by the capitalists is "technology will save us." A book that has been recommended to me, one which I recently checked out at the library and plan to get started on this weekend, is <i><a href="http://www.diamandis.com/abundance/">Abundance: The Future is Better Than You Think</a></i> (2012) by Peter Diamandis and <a href="http://www.stevenkotler.com/">Steven Kotler</a>. It seems manifestly like snake oil to me, this idea that we can come up with a quick and dirty tech solution to global system failure brought on by a couple of centuries of industrial capitalism; but I'll report back once I read the book.<br />
<br />
In any event, the Jamail piece has an impact on the way one looks at the world; it makes it hard to not look at the world as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manichaeism">Manichean</a>. There are forces of dark and there are forces of light. The purveyors of darkness want to maintain the current corrupt capitalist world order with its extraction of wealth and plenty for the 1%. They are arrayed against the rest of us, who while currently benighted at least have a yearning (in various stages of consciousness) for a little peace and calm.<br />
<br />
The United States as principal architect of our current doomed paradigm is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demiurge">demiurge</a>. But the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia is stepping up its devilish activities to fill the void the shrinking hyperpower leaves as it pivots to Asia to confront China.<br />
<br />
The latest installments in bloody Saudi skullduggery are in Lebanon. Anne Barnard, the Gray Lady's Beirut bureau chief, has a useful summary today, "<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/31/world/middleeast/lebanon-worried-about-border-fires-on-syrian-helicopters-in-its-airspace.html?ref=world">Lebanon, Worried About Border, Fires on Syrian Helicopters in Its Airspace</a>," of some of the recent spillover effects from the war in Syria. The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia recently announced that it has made a $3 billion purchase of the Lebanese Army:<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
On Sunday, the Lebanese president, Michel Suleiman, said the government had accepted a $3 billion aid package for the army from Saudi Arabia, an amount believed to be larger than the country&#8217;s defense budget.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
The move was welcomed by some as a way to strengthen the army, which is facing a growing array of threats from militants inside Lebanon. But it was viewed as provocative by supporters of Hezbollah, the Shiite militant group, who saw it as a bid to reduce the group&#8217;s influence over the army. Hezbollah, backed by Iran, supports the Syrian government, while Saudi Arabia is one of the main financiers of the Syrian insurgency.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Speculation arose Monday that the army&#8217;s new assertiveness against the Syrian airspace violation was at Saudi Arabia&#8217;s bidding in response to the aid package. Security officials said it was policy to respond to any violation of Lebanese territory. The army has also pursued and killed Syrian insurgents on Lebanese territory in recent weeks, according to the official National News Agency.</blockquote>
Couple this with a rocket fired Sunday, likely by Al Qaeda, into Israel from Lebanon (not to mention the series of terror bombings in Beirut) and the prodigious shadows of the sheikhs become visible. Isabel Kershner reporting from Jerusalem yesterday had the story, "<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/30/world/middleeast/rocket-fired-from-lebanon-lands-in-israel.html?hpw&amp;rref=world">Rocket Fire From Lebanon Prompts Shelling by Israel</a>":<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
There was no immediate claim of responsibility for the attack on Sunday, though those in recent years generally appear to have been the work of small militant groups in Lebanon rather than of Hezbollah.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
In August, four rockets were fired from Lebanon into Israel for the first time in two years. A militant group called the Brigades of Abdullah Azzam, an offshoot of Al Qaeda in Iraq, claimed responsibility. Israel responded to that attack by bombing what military officials here described as a &#8220;terrorist site&#8221; between the Lebanese cities of Beirut and Sidon.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
The Israeli defense minister, Moshe Yaalon, said Sunday that Israel would not tolerate fire from Lebanese territory and would use more force if necessary. &#8220;I do not recommend that anyone test our patience and our determination to preserve the security of Israeli citizens,&#8221; he said in a statement.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Notwithstanding the heightened tensions and harsh talk, an Israeli expert said that neither Israel nor Hezbollah, which is embroiled in the civil war in Syria, had an interest now in a full-blown confrontation on the Israeli-Lebanese front.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Aviv Oreg, a former head of the Al Qaeda and Global Jihad desk in Israel&#8217;s military intelligence department, told reporters that some kind of radical Islamic &#8220;global jihad&#8221; organization was probably behind the rocket fire on Sunday and that the attackers might have been trying to provoke Israel into retaliating against Hezbollah, since jihadist groups and Hezbollah are fighting on opposite sides of the conflict in Syria.</blockquote>
Nonetheless Netanyahu, working seamlessly per usual with his Saudi allies, blamed Hezbollah of war crimes.<br />
<br />
As the planet pitches toward a methane burp of "great dying," the Saudis are moving full speed ahead with a "smash and grab" of the Middle East.

<div class='post hentry' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-w0MMiu-x7-Y/UsCCddD1x4I/AAAAAAAAA5M/5wOq1pphI6o/s640/WhereMonstersDwell%2319c.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://10bennettave.blogspot.com/2013/12/where-monsters-dwell-19-insect-man.html'>Where Monsters Dwell #19: The Insect Man</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2390953041284571995' itemprop='description articleBody'>
"<a href="http://marvel.wikia.com/Where_Monsters_Dwell_Vol_1_19#Appearing_in_.22The_Insect_Man.22">The Insect Man</a>" originally appeared in the&nbsp;<a href="http://marvel.wikia.com/Tales_of_Suspense_Vol_1_24"><i>Tales of Suspense</i>&nbsp;#24</a>&nbsp;(December 1961). It is classic <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_Age_of_Comic_Books">Silver Age</a> science fiction by <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/comic-riffs/post/stan-lee-turns-91-we-salute-the-marvel-legend-with-this-animated-interview-video/2013/12/27/3d79863e-6ee5-11e3-aecc-85cb037b7236_blog.html">Stan Lee</a> and <a href="http://www.avclub.com/article/a-beginners-guide-to-the-king-of-comics-jack-kirby-102227">Jack Kirby</a>. Kirby's art is beautiful, much stronger than Lee's story, which includes elements Lee uses elsewhere: a subterranean capsule and a "It must have all just been a dream" ending.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://marvel.wikia.com/Where_Monsters_Dwell_Vol_1_19"><i>Where Monsters Dwell</i> #19</a>&nbsp;(January 1973) is noteworthy because it features original cover art by the great <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gil_Kane">Gil Kane</a>, Marvel's go-to cover artist in the early <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age_of_Comic_Books">Bronze Age</a>.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-w0MMiu-x7-Y/UsCCddD1x4I/AAAAAAAAA5M/5wOq1pphI6o/s1600/WhereMonstersDwell%2319c.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-w0MMiu-x7-Y/UsCCddD1x4I/AAAAAAAAA5M/5wOq1pphI6o/s640/WhereMonstersDwell%2319c.jpg" width="436" /></a></div>
<br />
In "The Insect Man" a U.S. Army sergeant is sent far underground in a space capsule as part of a preparedness program for a flight to the moon. The brass is worried about man's ability to survive long stretches alone. Putting a man in a capsule and dropping the capsule far below the surface of the earth is a way to gauge his suitability for space flight.<br />
<br />
Once underground the sergeant is captured by a giant race of insects. He becomes a plaything for an adolescent giant insect. Right when the sergeant is about to be vivisected by the curious giant insects he makes his getaway. Back on the surface the military brass assure him that the giant insects must have been a hallucination brought about by his isolation.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hbA_4L-8vYE/UsCCUqQMseI/AAAAAAAAA5E/9VV3nQQ8SWY/s1600/WhereMonstersDwell%2319p1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hbA_4L-8vYE/UsCCUqQMseI/AAAAAAAAA5E/9VV3nQQ8SWY/s640/WhereMonstersDwell%2319p1.jpg" width="430" /></a></div>
<br />
These Lee-Kirby scifi stories from the early 1960s all seem very quaint now with their preoccupation with monsters from outer space or from deep underground. Nowadays science is far more scary than any fiction we could dream up. Take for example <a href="http://dahrjamail.net/">Dahr Jamail</a>'s "<a href="http://www.tomdispatch.com/blog/175785/tomgram%3A_dahr_jamail%2C_the_climate_change_scorecard/">Are We Falling Off the Climate Precipice</a>," an article that appeared on <a href="http://www.tomdispatch.com/">TomDispatch</a>.com earlier this month and that generated some interest. In it Jamail assesses the current predictions of some well-established climate scientists who are a little more pessimistic than their colleagues.<br />
<br />
Jamail mostly focuses on what happens to the planet when the methane and carbon that is trapped in the Arctic is released into the atmosphere as the permafrost&nbsp;thaws, as it is now. Here is a sample:<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
How serious is the potential global methane build-up? <a href="http://www.sfgate.com/news/science/article/Federal-study-warns-of-sudden-climate-change-woes-5031607.php">Not all scientists</a> think it&#8217;s an immediate threat or even the major threat we face, but Ira Leifer, an atmospheric and marine scientist at the University of California, Santa Barbara, and one of the authors of the recent Arctic Methane study pointed out to me that &#8220;the Permian mass extinction that occurred 250 million years ago is related to methane and thought to be the key to what caused the extinction of most species on the planet.&#8221; In that extinction episode, it is estimated that 95% of all species were wiped out.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Also known as &#8220;The Great Dying,&#8221; it was triggered by a massive lava flow in an area of Siberia that led to an increase in global temperatures of six degrees Celsius. That, in turn, caused the melting of frozen methane deposits under the seas.  Released into the atmosphere, it caused temperatures to skyrocket further. All of this occurred over a period of approximately 80,000 years.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
We are currently in the midst of what scientists consider the sixth mass extinction in planetary history, with between 150 and 200 <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/environment/2010/aug/16/nature-economic-security">species</a> going extinct daily, a pace 1,000 times greater than the &#8220;natural&#8221; or &#8220;background&#8221; extinction rate. This event may already be comparable to, or even exceed, both the speed and intensity of the Permian mass extinction. The difference being that ours is human caused, isn&#8217;t going to take 80,000 years, has so far lasted just a few centuries, and is now gaining speed in a non-linear fashion.&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
It is possible that, on top of the vast quantities of carbon dioxide from fossil fuels that continue to enter the atmosphere in <a href="http://www.livescience.com/41326-2013-carbon-emissions-record-levels.html">record amounts</a> yearly, an increased release of methane could signal the beginning of the sort of process that led to the Great Dying. Some scientists fear that the situation is already so serious and so many self-reinforcing feedback loops are already in play that we are in the process of causing our own extinction. Worse yet, some are convinced that it could happen far more quickly than generally believed possible -- even in the course of just the next few decades.</blockquote>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-t7LKR6vV89o/UsCDBpiKJLI/AAAAAAAAA5Y/W1tnrGdzKLw/s1600/WhereMonstersDwell%2319p2a.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="338" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-t7LKR6vV89o/UsCDBpiKJLI/AAAAAAAAA5Y/W1tnrGdzKLw/s640/WhereMonstersDwell%2319p2a.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-h-RjZAL83Ts/UsCDBe6KjfI/AAAAAAAAA5U/KMOedcJXgVU/s1600/WhereMonstersDwell%2319p2b.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="304" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-h-RjZAL83Ts/UsCDBe6KjfI/AAAAAAAAA5U/KMOedcJXgVU/s640/WhereMonstersDwell%2319p2b.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-20_kNIK73eI/UsCDE_9RVRI/AAAAAAAAA5k/Ewvc2Av7EyU/s1600/WhereMonstersDwell%2319p2c.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="304" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-20_kNIK73eI/UsCDE_9RVRI/AAAAAAAAA5k/Ewvc2Av7EyU/s640/WhereMonstersDwell%2319p2c.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
Read Jamail's article. It is a clear-eyed appraisal of approaching system failure. Capitalism is of course killing us. We're not even listening to our scientists anymore. Here is how Jamail puts it:<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Not surprisingly, scientists with such views are often not the most popular guys in the global room. McPherson [<a href="http://guymcpherson.com/">Guy McPherson</a>, professor emeritus of evolutionary biology, natural resources, and ecology at the University of Arizona and a climate change expert of 25 years], for instance, has often been labeled &#8220;Guy McStinction&#8221; -- to which he responds, &#8220;I&#8217;m just reporting the results from other scientists. Nearly all of these results are published in established, esteemed literature. I don&#8217;t think anybody is taking issue with NASA, or Nature, or Science, or the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences. [Those] and the others I report are reasonably well known and come from legitimate sources, like NOAA [the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration], for example. I&#8217;m not making this information up, I&#8217;m just connecting a couple of dots, and it&#8217;s something many people have difficulty with.&#8221;&nbsp;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
McPherson does not hold out much hope for the future, nor for a governmental willingness to make anything close to the radical changes that would be necessary to quickly ease the flow of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere; nor does he expect the mainstream media to put much effort into reporting on all of this because, as he says, &#8220;There&#8217;s not much money in the end of civilization, and even less to be made in human extinction.&#8221; The destruction of the planet, on the other hand, is a good bet, he believes, &#8220;because there is money in this, and as long as that&#8217;s the case, it is going to continue.&#8221;</blockquote>
