<h3>Hopeless For O'Hanlon</h3>



<p>War forever promoter Michael O&#39;Hanlon pens another OpEd for the neoconned Washington Post opinion page: <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/us-intelligence-is-too-pessimistic-about-afghanistan/2014/01/02/eacde5a6-719b-11e3-9389-09ef9944065e_story.html">Hope For Afghanistan</a>. He suggests that <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/afghanistan-gains-will-be-lost-quickly-after-drawdown-us-intelligence-estimate-warns/2013/12/28/ac609f90-6f32-11e3-aecc-85cb037b7236_story.html">recent pessimistic intelligence estimates</a> about Afghanistan are wrong:</p>
<blockquote>The case for <strong>hope</strong>fulness on Afghanistan is built largely on what were probably its three most notable developments of 2013 ...</blockquote>
<p>O&#39;Hanlon&#39;s track record of predictions about the war on Afghanistan (and on Iraq) is not supporting his case. &quot;Hope&quot; does not win wars. &quot;Hope&quot; does not successfully occupy. Still &quot;hope&quot; is the only thing O&#39;Hanlon ever seems to have to promote further losses in already lost wars.</p>
<p>A casual and incomplete search for &quot;O&#39;Hanlon&quot; &quot;Hope&quot; &quot;Afghanistan&quot; finds the following entries:</p>
<p>TwinCities.com, November 17 2009: <a href="http://www.twincities.com/opinion/ci_13801672">Michael O&#39;Hanlon: A blue line of <strong>hope</strong> in Afghanistan</a></p>
<p>LA Times, December 27 2009: <a href="http://articles.latimes.com/2009/dec/27/opinion/la-oe-ohanlon27-2009dec27/2">A year of war -- and progress</a></p>
<blockquote>The question is whether it will be too little too late, but there is reason for <strong>hope</strong>.</blockquote>
<p>Washington Post, June 26 2010: <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/06/25/AR2010062504126.html">Reasons to be <strong>hope</strong>ful about U.S. strategy in Afghanistan</a></p>
<p>Politico, September 28 2010: <a href="http://www.politico.com/news/stories/0910/42781.html">New reasons for <strong>hope</strong> in Afghanistan</a></p>
<p>NYT, May 20 2011: <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/05/21/opinion/21ohanlon.html?_r=1&amp;">Finally, a Fighting Force</a></p>
<blockquote>But there is reason to be <strong>hop</strong>eful. ...</blockquote>
<p>CNN, March 16 2012: <a href="http://globalpublicsquare.blogs.cnn.com/2012/05/16/ohanlon-7-reasons-for-hope-in-afghanistan/">O&#39;Hanlon: 7 reasons for <strong>hope</strong> in Afghanistan</a></p>
<blockquote>Here are some highlights of the more hopeful indicators in Afghanistan: ...</blockquote>
<p>CNN, May 2 2012: <a href="http://globalpublicsquare.blogs.cnn.com/2012/05/02/ohanlon-reasons-for-hope-on-afghanistan/">O&#39;Hanlon: Reasons for <strong>hope</strong> on Afghanistan</a></p>
<p>Washington Times, June 1 2012: <a href="http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2012/jun/1/rays-of-hope-in-afghanistan/">Oâ€™HANLON: Rays of <strong>hope</strong> in Afghanistan</a></p>
<blockquote>Several hopeful things I saw on a recent trip ...</blockquote>
<p>Politico, March 21 2013: <a href="http://www.politico.com/story/2013/03/kandahar-and-hope-89147.html">Kandahar and <strong>hope</strong></a></p>
<blockquote>Now, Kandahar gives hope to the war effort. ...</blockquote>
<p>One wonders why the above media feel that they still have to promote the nonsense of such a hopelessly delusional idiot?</p>
